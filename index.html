Persistent Friendship Protocol (PFP)

A structured protocol for maintaining long-term group friendship cohesion under distance, time fragmentation, and coordination friction. Designed to resist friendship entropy through recurring narrative reinforcement. How to go about this: Read the objectives, and follow each tab one after the other.

Objective

Preserve group identity, mutual relevance, and emotional accessibility across time. The end goal: no member becomes socially foreign to the group despite physical separation.

Problem

Friendship entropy occurs when shared narrative reinforcement drops below decay rate. Coordination friction accelerates this decay.

Core Definitions

  • Cost: Cognitive + emotional + temporal effort required to contribute.
  • Activation Energy: Psychological resistance threshold before participation begins.
  • Friendship Entropy: Natural erosion of familiarity and narrative continuity over time.
  • Signal: Meaningful update that advances shared narrative or reveals internal state.
  • Reciprocity: Balanced participation measured over long time intervals.
  • Identity Cohesion: Degree to which members perceive belonging to an ongoing collective.

Implementation Protocol

  • Establish a persistent communication channel.
  • Normalize meaningful narrative signals.
  • Reference past context to build density.
  • Maintain asynchronous norms to reduce friction.
  • Schedule periodic high-bandwidth sync sessions.

Contribution Requirements

  • Life transitions
  • Internal reflections
  • Progress on ambitions
  • Struggles and vulnerability
  • Shared memory callbacks

Contributions must increase narrative depth or emotional visibility. Filler communication does not sustain cohesion.

Failure Modes

  • Chronic asymmetry in contribution
  • Unresolved interpersonal tension
  • Signal overload fatigue
  • Value divergence exceeding shared identity
  • Ritualization without authenticity

Abstract System Model

Friendship behaves as a dynamic equilibrium system. If reinforcement frequency drops below entropy rate, cohesion decays. If activation energy exceeds participation threshold, contribution collapses.
